# Political Party Reference Guide

## Overview

This document provides a comprehensive guide to the major U.S. political parties' values, constituencies, and positions. It's intended to help journalists and editors accurately represent different perspectives.

---

## Democratic Party

### Core Values

- **Economic**: Support for government intervention in economy, social safety nets, workers' rights
- **Social**: Civil rights, equality, diversity, progressive social policies
- **Environmental**: Climate action, environmental regulation, sustainability
- **Government Role**: Active government involvement in addressing social problems

### Key Constituencies

- **Urban voters**: Major cities tend to vote Democratic
- **Young voters**: Ages 18-44 historically lean Democratic
- **Minority communities**: Black, Hispanic, Asian American voters
- **College-educated voters**: Especially in suburban areas
- **Women**: Particularly on social issues
- **Labor unions**: Traditional union support
- **LGBTQ+ community**: Strong advocacy for rights
- **Environmental advocates**: Climate-focused voters

### Typical Positions

| Issue | Democratic Position |
|-------|---------------------|
| Healthcare | Expand access (ACA, Medicare for All options) |
| Climate/Energy | Renewable energy, regulation, Paris Agreement |
| Immigration | Path to citizenship, refugee support |
| Taxes | Progressive taxation, close loopholes |
| Education | Public education funding, student debt relief |
| Criminal Justice | Reform, reduce mass incarceration |
| Guns | Background checks, some regulation |

---

## Republican Party

### Core Values

- **Economic**: Free market, limited government, low taxes, deregulation
- **Social**: Traditional values, individual responsibility, religious freedom
- **Constitutional**: Originalism, federalism, states' rights
- **National Security**: Strong military, border security

### Key Constituencies

- **Rural voters**: Countryside areas
- **Older voters**: Ages 45+ tend to lean Republican
- **White voters without college**: Especially in Midwest/South
- **Business community**: Pro-business policies
- **Evangelical Christians**: Religious right
- **Gun owners**: Strong 2nd Amendment support
- **Veterans**: Military and national security

### Typical Positions

| Issue | Republican Position |
|-------|---------------------|
| Healthcare | Market-based solutions, repeal/replace ACA |
| Climate/Energy | Energy independence, skeptical of Green New Deal |
| Immigration | Border security, strict enforcement |
| Taxes | Lower taxes, simplification, supply-side |
| Education | School choice, local control |
| Criminal Justice | Law and order, tough on crime |
| Guns | Strong 2nd Amendment, resist restrictions |
